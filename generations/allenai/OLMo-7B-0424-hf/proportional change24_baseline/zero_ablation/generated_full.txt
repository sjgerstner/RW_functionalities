Ngāra tātā ā motuhake

Ngāra tātā ā motuhake means "That which is present in the present's being." 
Academically, it means "That which is hidden in the object of thinking itself." 
In philosophy, it relates to the "thing-in-being", and you can find "na ž槚" in Phileinos, The Shepherd and Amazons, Three-Soldier Line, and The Prodigal Son.
